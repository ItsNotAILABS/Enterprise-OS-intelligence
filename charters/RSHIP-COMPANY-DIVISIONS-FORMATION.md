### The Single Parent, Multiple Divisions Model

RSHIP is **one company** with **specialized divisions** that focus on distinct markets. Every division:

1. **Shares the RSHIP Framework** (the common substrate)
2. **Uses RSHIP-designated AGI systems** (from the common SDK catalog)
3. **Reports to the parent entity** (Medina Tech)
4. **Maintains its own P&L, team, and go-to-market**
5. **Cross-licenses AGI capabilities** from other divisions as needed

This structure enables:
- **Deep domain specialization** — a Skyhi aviation expert isn't distracted by healthcare compliance
- **Shared infrastructure cost** — all divisions share the RSHIP Framework, AEGIX orchestration, and Cloudflare Worker infrastructure
- **Cross-division value** — a TRACTEX capability built for enterprise operations is immediately available to aviation, healthcare, and construction
- **Independent growth** — each division can grow at its own pace without slowing the others

## How the Divisions Work Together

### The RSHIP AGI Marketplace

Every AGI in the SDK catalog is available to every division. When Division 2 (Enterprise) needs crew scheduling for a hospital, it licenses CREWEX from Division 1. When Division 1 (Skyhi) needs predictive analytics for route planning, it uses PRAEDEX from Division 2's stack.

This creates a **compound value effect** — each new AGI built by any division increases the capabilities available to all divisions.

### Concrete Cross-Division Synergies

| Division 1 Skyhi | Borrows From | Division 2 |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------|
| SECUREX (TSA checkpoint) | → | SECUREX also used in DFW enterprise app |
| COMMUNEX (aerotropolis) | → | COMMUNEX used in airport community product |
| OPEREX (workflow templates) | → | OPEREX powers enterprise onboarding across all products |

| Division 1 Skyhi | Builds For | Division 4 |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------|
| CREWEX | → | Open-source FRMS for small carriers (ecosystem adoption) |
| VISITEX | → | Public API for regional airports who can't afford enterprise |

### The AEGIX Layer (Division 3)

When Skyhi deploys all 5 AGIs at DFW, AEGIX (Division 3) becomes the meta-orchestrator that:
- Monitors heartbeats of all 5 AGIs
- Detects Byzantine failures (if TRAVEX becomes degraded)
- Routes AGI-to-AGI messages via the AGI Message Bus
- Auto-restarts failed AGI organs

This means Division 3's infrastructure is **always underneath** every Division 1 and Division 2 deployment — invisible to the customer, essential to the platform.

### Revenue Flow

```
Customer Pays Division 1/2 (product revenue)
        │
        ├─ Division 3 receives internal transfer
        │   (infrastructure cost allocation: ~15%)
        │
        └─ Division 4 receives brand attribution
            (ecosystem adoption → enterprise conversion pipeline)
```
